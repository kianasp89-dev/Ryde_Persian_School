About Us

Ryde Persian School is a not-for-profit organization dedicated to teaching the Persian language to learners of all ages. The school operates under the umbrella of the Iranian Educational and Cultural Centre Inc.

Established in 1988, Ryde Persian School was founded to support Persian Australian children in the Ryde area and to ensure that the Persian language and cultural traditions are passed on to future generations. The Persian community has made significant contributions to Ryde’s economic, cultural, artistic, and social life.

Ryde Persian School proudly celebrates this shared heritage and believes that recognizing and valuing diverse community histories strengthens understanding, inclusion, and connection across the wider Australian society.

The aims of Ryde Persian School are to teach the Persian language to children from preschool through to high school; to celebrate and promote the cultural heritage of the Persian community in Australia, regardless of race, religious beliefs, or political views; to organize cultural events and ceremonies that engage students and their families; and to strengthen students’ confidence in using the Persian language and actively engaging with Persian culture.

Board Members

The Board Members of Ryde Persian School are responsible for the governance, control, and management of the affairs of the School in accordance with the Constitution and the Associations Incorporation Act 2009 (NSW).

The Board Members act collectively to oversee the strategic direction of the School, ensure proper management of its operations and finances, and uphold the objectives of Ryde Persian School, including the promotion and preservation of Persian language and cultural education.

Board Members serve in a voluntary capacity and exercise their powers and responsibilities in the best interests of the School, its members, and its students, in compliance with the Constitution and applicable laws.
